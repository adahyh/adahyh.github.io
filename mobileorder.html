Triton2Go Redesign

Transforming UCSD's Mobile Food Ordering App


Timeline:
10 weeks
Tools
Figma
Role:
UIUX Designer, Researcher

Overview

For UC San Diego students, food ordering is made convenient through Triton2Go, or Triton Mobile app. I worked in a team to discover possible problems with it and address these deficits to redesign the app in a high-fidelity prototype. Following further critiques and feedback, I chose to reiterate and create my own final design.


The Problem

While Triton2Go serves its purpose as a campus food ordering app, its issues with feature bloat, a lack of signifiers and essential information, and accidental navigations create confusion among users. For example, interviews revealed users experienced having to restart their entire order due to one mistake.


The Solution

Our solution was to redesign the app keeping the students' needs in mind and centering the redesign around the most used or coveted features. Users primarily perceived the app as a food-ordering platform, so we wanted to cater to this and fit the app to the users' mental models of a food ordering app. Additionally, issues with a lack of information, such as not displaying funds or having pictures of food, would be addressed to prevent frustration.
