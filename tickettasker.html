TicketTasker

Bridging Volunteerism and Music Festivals


Timeline:
10 weeks
Tools
Figma, FigJam, Google Slides & Sheets
Role:
UX Designer, Researcher

Overview

This project was made for COGS 123: Social Computing, a course at UC San Diego. My team and I sought to explore the intersection between social behavior and computational systems. This was done through creating a social computing experience that the students could participate in, and we decided to create TicketTasker: a way to bring together music festival volunteers who might attend by themselves, and hope to make friends with like-minded individuals who want to give back to the environment and while having fun.

My role in this project was a UX designer and researcher. I contributed to creating surveys and analyzing the data, performing literature review and researching the competitive space, and creating high fidelity prototypes.

The Problem

Imagine...
You want to attend a music festival with your friends!

But...
  1. You don't want to spend money.
    You're a broke college student, or you want to spend money on other important things. Music festival spending is not in your budget.
  2. Festivals are typically bad for the environment.
    This is because of air pollution, energy consumption, congested roads, etc. (Weston et al., 2015). They also often rely on cheaper exploitative labor to clean up after.
  3. Cheap tickets are hard to find.
    Yes, there might be one-day passes that are significantly cheaper, but you l ove the lineup so much that you want to attend all 3 days of the festival. Or, you're traveling far and have to pay for housing and a plane ticket, so you might as well attend all festival days.
  4. Sometimes it isn't easy to find people to go with.
    The social aspect is the key motivating factor for engaging in leisure activities like attending music festivals. (O'Neill & Egermann, 2022). You also might have friends who are going and you don't want to have FOMO (fear of missing out).


The Solution

To enhance accessibility to attend music festivals while promoting sustainability and fostering social interactions at these events, we created TicketTasker. TicketTasker is an app that allows you to volunteer at music festivals with friends or by yourself in exchange for free tickets.

App Overview

Homepage:
Available music festivals are listed by their event tier. Based on responses from our prototyping session, users were motivated by the gamification aspects of the app. We made sure to continue incorporating these aspects through presenting, but keeping higher tier events locked.
My Events:
Users can view the events that they have signed up to volunteer at.
Messages:
Users can message one another to get to know each other or find friends to attend the festival or volunteer with.
My Account:
A profile page for the user, with information such as their name, rating, and social media which can be viewed by other volunteers and task organizers. The user can access their personal information such as favorited events, past reviews by task organizers, and their deposit.

Event Sign-Up & Sharing
Users can select an event on the homepage, which will lead them to the event specific page where they can view information including a description, past ratings by other volunteers, and the event attendees. From there, they can sign up for the event, which involves choosing their shift time and volunteer task, filling out personal information, and placing their deposit.

While we hope to flesh out a feature in the future that allows people to see which of their friends sign up for events directly on the sign-up page, we currently have a feature that allows friends to share in the app or on iMessage after they sign up for an event. Our hope is that this feature will encourage more people to engage with the app or download it if they receive a link from a friend encouraging them to sign up together.


Message a Friend
Users can interact with one another through texting in event group chats, containing every volunteer from the event, or by private messaging specific volunteers. This gives them the opportunity to make friends or speak before volunteering together.


Event Check-in and Check-out
The app is used by both event volunteers and event organizers, who are running the music festival.

  Volunteer
After arriving at the event, users are given the option to check-in on the event page. From there, their app will open a camera where they can scan the check-in QR code that the organizer will present. The check-out process is identical, but users will instead select check-out.

  Organizer
Once the event starts, the Task Organizer receives a QR code. This is presented to volunteers to scan so that they can be checked-in to the event. The same process is done for checking-out. Task Organizers are also given the option to view all volunteers, which can be sorted by their task and shift time. This presents information on the volunteer such as their check-in/check-out times, their entire volunteer schedule, and a place to rate the volunteer.


Next Steps


Our aim was to develop a mobile app that cultivates a social computing atmosphere encompassing the phases before, during, and after attending a music festival. Through our research, we recognized that crafting distinctive social interactions within the context of music festivals necessitates continuous iterations and feedback to discern what drives users to engage with our app. We prioritized implementing reward systems and gamification while consciously avoiding exploitative social computing strategies. Our aspiration is for our social media application to facilitate online communication, transitioning plans from group chats into real-life experiences, thus motivating individuals to actively embrace offline living. Some of the next steps we hope to see in the next phase of our app are:


Fleshing Out & Adding More Features
We would hope to flesh out the volunteer and task organizer review system a bit more, especially with the appeal process for a bad review, the check out process for users, and we'd want to find a way to highlight when prospective volunteers' friends are volunteering when they're signing up. We'd also try and figure out a way for users to be held accountable without having to “pay” for a ticket.


Further the Gamification Aspect
In the future, we would also like to further develop the gamification aspect, where different tasks earn more points. For example, less desirable tasks such as trash pickup can offer more points to incentivize users to sign up for these unwanted tasks. We would also love to show people what tier their friends are at and highlight exclusive opportunities that come with different tiers to encourage healthy competition.
