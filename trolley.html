Pronto Trolley Transportation

Integrating Trolley-Centered Features onto a Transit App


Timeline:
10 weeks
Tools
Figma
Role:
UX Developer and Researcher

Overview

This project was done for a class at UCSD called Interaction Design. This class emphasized the design process and developing solutions for needs that users might not have even realized they had. I went through the phases of observations and need finding, to card-sorting and ideation, and finally prototyping and developing a website. After observing the experience of trolley riders on the UC San Diego Blue Line, I decided on that they would be my target group to focus my project on.


Observational Research

I began my observations with this guiding research question:
How do riders navigate the trolley and use its components?

Through following the research question, different avenues could be explored. Components of the trolley included the trolley's exterior and interior, the platforms, the pass scanners or payment options for trolley.

After observing riders on the platforms and in the trolley cars, I was able to determine common behaviors that led to three main observed needs:

  1. Reduced Congestion by Doors:
    Riders were trying to exit while others were trying to enter. There could be more clear indicators of boarding and disembarking.

  2. Knowledge of Next Station and Exit Points:
    Users had other navigation apps open throughout the ride, and would frequently check the map.

  3. Pass Scanning Options:
    Many riders would rush to get on the trolley and not stop at the platform to physically scan their trolley pass before boarding.

The observed needs were narrowed down to these two need statements to drive the rest of my project:
  1. Riders need to be able to tell where and when to enter or exit the trolley.
  2. Riders need to be able to pay their fare without requiring a specific device.
